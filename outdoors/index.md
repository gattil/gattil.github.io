When I'm not at a keyboard or in a cockpit, I'm usually outside — on a bike or on a mountain.

### On the bike

I ride a road bike out of Zurich. Most weeks that's the local climbs — the Albis, the Whiskypass — but the days I remember are the big alpine ones: the long haul over the Gotthard pass, around 88 km and more than 2,300 m of climbing in a single day.

### In the mountains

On foot I'm happiest high up. I hike the Swiss Alps and the Jura — the Uetliberg above Zurich on an ordinary day, bigger summits past 3,000 m when I can. I keep a quiet tally of the peaks I've stood on; the climbing is the point, not the speed.
